FOOD CROPS

CASH CROPS

Cash crops are planted for the purpose of selling on the market/export to make profit, as distinguished from subsistence crops planted for the purpose of self-supply of the Farmer.

FRUIT CROPS

Fruit crops produce a higher yield per unit area of land than any other agronomic crop, regardless of the crop type.

NITROGEN

Nitrogen is a major component of chlorophyll,by which plants use sunlight energy to produce sugars from water and carbon dioxide

PHOSPHOROUS

Phosphorus helps filter out waste in the kidneys and plays an essential role in how the body stores and uses energy.

POTASSIUM

Potassium plays a vital role in the translocation of sugars and starch from the leaves through to storage organs such as fruits, bulbs and tubers.
